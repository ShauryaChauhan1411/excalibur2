BRAHMA TEMPLE,PUSHKAR

Brahma Temple is a famous Hindu temple located in Pushkar, Rajasthan, India, dedicated to Lord Brahma, the creator in Hindu mythology. It is one of the very few temples in the world devoted to Brahma, making it a unique spiritual destination. The temple, believed to have been built in the 14th century, showcases traditional Rajasthani architecture with a prominent red spire, intricately carved pillars, and a beautifully adorned sanctum. The idol of Lord Brahma is depicted with four faces, symbolizing his role as the creator of the universe. The temple is situated near the sacred Pushkar Lake, where devotees perform holy rituals and take ceremonial dips during festivals, especially during the annual Pushkar Camel Fair and Kartik Poornima. The temple attracts pilgrims, tourists, and photographers alike, offering a serene spiritual ambiance.
